HONORS SEMINARS - FALL 2020

About

The UT Honors and Scholars Programs offer a variety of engagement opportunities to be involved and engaged.

These are framed into 5 categories named the "5 C's", and are Curiosity, Culture, Connection, Community, and Campus.

Members of Honors & Scholars are passionate about learning, both inside and outside the classroom. Curiosity inspires scholars to explore their academic interests through engaging and rewarding co-curricular experiences. These experiences vary widely in topic and form, offering students flexibility and variability in choosing the right mix of experiences for them.

BlueSky Statistics Workshop

- Office of Information Technology (OIT) - Presenter: Bob Muenchen - Date: Friday, November 6 at 10:00am to 12:00pm

BlueSky is a free and open source data science package with extensive features for data management, visualization, statistical analysis, and machine learning. Its point-and-click user interface is easy to learn and use. Behind the scenes it uses the powerful R language. If you wish to learn R programming, you can ask BlueSky to show you the R code that it writes. It uses the popular ggplot R package for visualization and tidyverse commands for data management.

Mr. Muenchen is the author of "BlueSky Statistics User's Guide" as well as the "R for SAS and SPSS Users"

LEADing with Confidencece

- Educational Leadership and Policy Studies - Presenters: Karen D. Boyd and Megan King - Date: Tuesday, November 3 at 6:00pm to 7:00pm

Impostor syndrome is the belief that one's successes are the product of luck or fraud rather than skill. In this UT LEAD program you will gain a better understanding of the symptoms, causes, and effects of impostor syndrome. Explore the roots of impostor syndrome and strategies that can be employed to combat feelings of selfdoubt and inadequacy so that you can lead with confidence.
